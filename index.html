About Me

I am a Study Coordinator and Clinical Research Assistant in Dr. Anthony Kingâ€™s Mindfulness Lab at The Ohio State University Department of Psychiatry and Behavioral Health. Over the past two years, I have gained training and hands-on experience in fMRI, EEG, neuropsychological and physiological testing, and clinical interviewing.

I have contributed to IRB submissions, grant writing, and manuscript preparation. My work bridges clinical practice and neuroscience, with a focus on how mindfulness-based interventions influence functional network connectivity and clinical outcomes in people with PTSD and Treatment-Resistant Depression.

I am broadly interested in utilizing multimodal methodologies integrating neuroimaging, behavioral, neurophysiological, and ecological measures to examine the neural and cognitive mechanisms underlying emotion regulation, self-referential processing, rumination, and autobiographical memory in mood disorders.

Previous Work

State of Ohio Adversity and Resiliency Study (SOAR)

  • I served as the first clinical research assistant for SOAR, a state-funded, state-wide, multi-generational, deep phenotyping study to uncover the biological, psychological, and social patterns related to mental health as well as modifiable risk and resiliency factors of mental illness in up to 3,600 participants. I performed all clinical activities with families, including multimodal fMRI, EEG, neuropsychological testing, EMA, and collection of blood biomarkers.
    • Learn more

Ketamine-Assisted Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy for Treatment-Resistant Depression

  • I served as the clincal assessor for the primary outcome measure, performing over 100 hours of the Montgomery-Asberg Depression Rating Scale (MADRS) for this study that combined one infusion of IV Ketamine with an 8-week Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy course. EEG, blood draws, and EMA were collected at three timepoints in addition to weekly MADRS assessments to assess for changes in depression symptoms severity post-infusion.

Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy for Posttraumatic Stress Disorder

  • This two-arm study assessed participants for PTSD and pandemic-related stress. Participants with PTSD participated in an 8-week Mindfulness-Based Cognitive-Therapy course and completed clinical interviews, questionnaires, fMRI, heart-rate variability, and saliva collection. Participants in the pandemic stress arm of the study, participated in an 8-week progressive muscle relaxation course, a mind-body technique.
