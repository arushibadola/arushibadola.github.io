Previous Work

State of Ohio Adversity and Resiliency Study (SOAR)

  • I served as the first clinical research assistant for SOAR, a state-funded, state-wide, multi-generational, deep phenotyping study to uncover the biological, psychological, and social patterns related to mental health as well as modifiable risk and resiliency factors of mental illness in up to 3,600 participants. I performed all clinical activities with families, including multimodal fMRI, EEG, neuropsychological testing, EMA, and collection of blood biomarkers.

Ketamine-Assisted Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy for Treatment-Resistant Depression

  • I served as the clincal assessor for the primary outcome measure, the Montgomery-Asberg Depression Rating Scale (MADRS) for this study that combined one infusion of IV Ketamine with an 8-week Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy course. EEG, blood draws, and EMA were collected at three timepoints in addition to weekly MADRS assessments to assess for changes in depression symptoms severity post-infusion.

Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy for Posttraumatic Stress Disorder

  • This two-arm study assessed participants for PTSD and pandemic-related stress. Participants with PTSD participated in an 8-week Mindfulness-Based Cognitive-Therapy course and completed clinical interviews, questionnaires, fMRI, and saliva collection. Participants in the pandemic stress arm of the study, participated in an 8-week progressive muscle relaxation course, a mind-body technique.
